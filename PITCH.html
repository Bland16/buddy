The problem

Most people never learn who they actually click with

  • Dating & networking apps are high-stakes and profile-first — you perform a curated self before a word is exchanged.
  • So conversations get judged on photos and bios, not on whether you two actually vibe.
  • People leave college having met thousands of peers but still can't name the type of person they connect with.

The gap

There's no low-stakes place to just talk to someone new and notice "oh — I really like talking to people like this."

That noticing is the product.

Buddy03
Engagement engine

Gift points you can't spend yourself

  • As you chat, you randomly earn gift points — but only your match can spend them, not you.
  • A small, charming twist that rewards showing up and being good company.
  • Points cash out in the Marketplace for real rewards — $5 gift cards and more.
Amazon $5Spotify StarbucksVenmo

Why it works

You can't farm your own rewards — so the only way to "win" is to have good conversations. Generosity, retention, and kindness all become the same action.

Buddy04
How we make money

Sponsored banner ads fund the gift cards

  • One sponsor at a time owns the chat wallpaper — a single brand tiled softly behind the conversation, rotating between sponsors.
  • Every appearance is logged as a CPM impression — standard banner ad economics.
  • That ad revenue underwrites the gift cards: brands pay to reach students; students get real rewards; we keep the spread.

Sponsors and gift-card merchants are the same brands students already love.

The flywheel

Ads → fund gift cards → drive chats → create ad impressions → more ads.

– Stripe + infra  ~$0.15

≈ +$1.15contribution margin / user / month

The structural edge

  • Our biggest variable cost — rewards — is a single config dial (the point-drop rate), so we cap payouts at a fixed % of ad revenue. Margin can't go negative at scale.
  • Reality check: Snapchat's North America ARPU is ~$7.67/quarter across its whole ad stack — so a single banner unit at ~$2.50/mo is conservative, with room to grow as we add formats.

Benchmarks: US display CPM $1–10 (programmatic ~$2, Google Display $2–5); Snap Q2-2024 NA ARPU $7.67. Internal model; assumptions configurable per market.
