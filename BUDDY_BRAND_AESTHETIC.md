# BUDDY — Brand & Aesthetic Instructions

> The single source of truth for how Buddy *sounds* and *feels*. Anyone (or any AI) writing copy,
> designing a screen, or generating art should follow this. Pair it with `BUDDY_SPRITE_SHEET_PROMPT.md`
> (visual assets) and `BUDDY_SCAFFOLD_PROMPT.md` (build).

---

## 1 · POSITIONING

**What Buddy is (the public story):**
A low-stakes place to talk to someone new — anonymously — and figure out **who you actually click with**.
No profiles to perfect, no names, no stakes. Just a conversation, and the quiet fun of noticing *"oh, I
like talking to people like this."*

**The promise, in one line:** *Find out who you click with.*

**The hidden goal (INTERNAL ONLY — never appears in any copy, ever):**
Buddy is a pressure-free room to get more comfortable and fluent in conversation. We never say this. The
moment Buddy reads as a "social skills" or "confidence" tool, people stop using it. We sell *curiosity
and connection*; the ease comes as a side effect.

**What Buddy is NOT (say so when it clarifies):**
- Not dating. Not networking. Not therapy. Not a self-improvement program.
- Not about meeting up IRL — the whole point is that it stays here, low-stakes.

---

## 2 · AUDIENCE

University students and new grads. Assume they are: smart, busy, fluent in internet, and **allergic to
being marketed to or talked down to**. They can smell try-hard and self-help from a mile away. Write for
a sharp friend, not a "user."

**Gender-neutral, always:**
- Default to **they/them**, "someone," "the other person," "your match," "people," "whoever you're
  talking to."
- Banned: "guys," "girls/boys," "ladies/gentlemen," "dude/man/bro," and any romantic assumption
  (no "match" in the dating sense — it just means the person you're paired with).

---

## 3 · VOICE — Warm & Low-Key

Talk like a thoughtful friend who's good at texting. Warm, plain, unhurried. The calm comes from *ease*,
not from softness-theater.

**Principles**
1. **Plain and short.** Real sentences a person would text. Cut the throat-clearing.
2. **Warmth through specificity, not hype.** No exclamation-mark confetti, no "Yay!", no "Let's gooo."
3. **Never coach, grade, or cheerlead.** No "you should," no "great job," no streak-guilt.
4. **Dry wit in small doses** is welcome — never sarcasm aimed at the user.
5. **Assume intelligence.** Don't over-explain. One idea per screen.
6. **Lowercase is fine** where it feels natural; don't force a gimmick either way.

**Do / Don't**
| Don't (try-hard / patronizing) | Do (warm & low-key) |
|---|---|
| "Practice your social skills in a safe space!" | "Talk to someone. See who clicks." |
| "Build confidence one chat at a time 💪" | "No names, no pressure. Just talk." |
| "Great job! You sent your first message!" | "Nice. That's a conversation." |
| "Don't be shy — everyone's friendly here!" | "Whenever you're ready." |
| "Join our community of connectors!" | "There's always someone around." |

---

## 4 · VOCABULARY

**Never use (keeps the hidden goal hidden + avoids patronizing):**
- *Growth/self-help:* practice, confidence, skills, improve, grow, journey, level up, unlock your
  potential, better version of you.
- *Therapy-speak:* safe space, vulnerable, healing, hold space, your feelings are valid.
- *Labels:* shy, awkward, introvert, lonely, anxious — never name the user as deficient.
- *Startup/corporate:* empower, community, connect (as a noun-y buzzword), leverage, synergy, engage.

**Prefer:**
- *click, vibe, talk, someone, your kind of people, low-key, no pressure, no names, whenever you want,
  see who you get on with, the people you actually like.*

**Quick swaps**
| Reaching for… | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "build confidence" | (drop it) "see who you click with" |
| "practice talking" | "just talk to someone" |
| "our community" | "the people here" / "everyone around" |
| "connect with people" | "talk to people" / "meet someone" |
| "safe space" | "low-key" / "no pressure" |

## 5 · MICROCOPY PATTERNS (rewritten with the new framing)

These replace the old "Practice being social. No pressure, no names." line and any "introvert/confidence"
copy in the scaffold and sprite docs.

- **Tagline (primary):** *Find out who you click with.*
  Alternates: *No names. Just good conversations.* · *Talk to someone. See who clicks.* · *Meet your
  kind of person.*
- **Landing CTA:** `Start talking` (not "Sign up to grow your social life").
- **Onboarding (goals survey) — reframe from "social goals" to taste/curiosity:**
  - Header: *"What kind of conversations are you into?"*
  - Options as vibes, not improvements: "deep dives," "easy banter," "niche obsessions," "anything,
    honestly."
- **Matchmaking wait:** *"Finding someone to talk to…"* (Buddy sleepy/waiting sprite). Never "Finding
  you a practice partner."
- **Chat empty state:** *"Say anything. There's no wrong opener here."*
- **Moderation nudge (warm, never preachy, gentle Buddy sprite):**
  *"Quick one — let's keep names, numbers, and socials out of it. All good to keep going?"*
  (Frames it as the vibe of the place, not a rule you broke. No "violation," no "warning.")
- **Gift point drop:** *"You picked up a gift point — it's for [match] to spend, not you."* Keep the
  twist charming, not gamified-cheesy. (Deep-green coin sprite.)
- **Marketplace:** *"Points your matches gave you. Spend them on something real."* Items listed plainly,
  no "Redeem your rewards journey!"
- **Profile:** show what they like / who they've clicked with, framed as *taste*, not *progress*. Avoid
  scores, levels, or "confidence meters."

**Error / edge copy stays human:** *"That didn't send — try again?"* not "Error 500."

## 6 · VISUAL AESTHETIC — Expressive, but minimalist

The look = **Partiful/Lapse character dialed into a clean, minimal frame.** Personality lives in *type,
the Buddy mascot, and the mint plaid* — but the layouts are calm, white, and uncluttered. Restraint is
the brand. (Full palette + sprite rules live in `BUDDY_SPRITE_SHEET_PROMPT.md`.)

**Layout**
- White / off-white (`#F4FAF6`) canvas, *generous* whitespace, one clear action per screen.
- Type-forward: let a confident headline carry the screen; minimal chrome around it.
- Mint (`#5FBF86`) is the single brand color — primary buttons and key moments. Pine green (`#1F4A36`)
  for text. Deep green (`#3E9B68`) for small accents. That's it — no other hues.

**Type**
- Pairing: a **rounded geometric sans** (Quicksand / Nunito / Baloo 2) for a soft, friendly read.
  Headlines big and warm; body calm and small. One or two weights — keep it clean.

**The mascot (Buddy) — earns its appearances**
- Buddy is punctuation and delight, **not decoration on every screen.** Use it for: the wink/smirk logo,
  empty states, the wait screen, the moderation nudge, and celebration moments (point drop, redemption).
- Don't crowd UI with mascot poses. One Buddy per moment, lots of space around it.

**The plaid — texture, used sparingly**
- The soft mint gingham is an *accent*, not full wallpaper: a faint header band, a card backdrop, an
  empty-state field. Keep it low-opacity and **never behind body text.** It should whisper "cozy," not
  shout.

**Motion**
- Gentle and minimal: soft fades, a light squish/bob on Buddy. Calm, never bouncy-frantic. Delight
  moments (point drop) can have one small, satisfying pop — then settle.

**Feel checklist (every screen should pass):**
- Could a sharp 22-year-old screenshot this without cringing? ✅
- Does it look calm and uncluttered at a glance? ✅
- Is there exactly one obvious thing to do? ✅
- Did we avoid every banned word? ✅
